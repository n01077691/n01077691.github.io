Week 11

    Progress Report
  • On November 12 we had Open House Event at North Campus where we had to present our project. Our goal was to establish connection between 2 Nordic semiconductors (SparkFun nRf2401+ breakout boards) and display it on Ardunino's serial monitor. We have successfully established the connection and received some feedback. Also, we added a feature of LED communication through the code. In other words, When the connection between 2 boards was established (delay 5 seconds), LED of sender goes green. Otherwise, if connection fails out, the LED goes red. LED of the receiver is always green because it is indicating that the board is always trying to receive a signal.

    This is Q/A from Open House:

    Q: Is everything built from scratch and how did you assemble it?
    A: Drone package included the quadcopter and controller already. We had to build the rest ourselves.

    Q: Which type of connection is going between 2 boards?
    A: SPI (Serial Peripheral Interface). It is serial communication interface used to communicate in short distances.

    Q: What will be the final state of the project?
    A: Full control on the drone by the software (Android Application).

  • Right now we are analyzing packets that are getting send to oscilloscope when we push different buttons on the controller. Controller's PCB has embedded transceiver IC which is problematic because we were expecting to use IC separately from the controller's PCB.
